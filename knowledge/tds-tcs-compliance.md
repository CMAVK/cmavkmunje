# TDS and TCS — Obligations, Due Dates and Documents

## When TDS applies (common situations)

- Salary payments to employees
- Rent above the prescribed annual limit
- Payments to contractors and sub-contractors
- Professional or technical fees
- Interest, commission and brokerage
- Purchase of property above ₹50 lakh (buyer must deduct)
- Purchase of goods above the prescribed limit for larger buyers

Rates and thresholds change with the law — we confirm the exact section and
rate for each payment type before you deduct.

## Key deadlines (typical pattern — confirm current dates with us)

- Deposit of TDS deducted: by the 7th of the following month (30 April for March)
- Quarterly TDS returns: within the month following each quarter
- TDS certificates (Form 16/16A): due after each quarterly return; Form 16 annually for salary

## Consequences of default

Interest applies for late deduction and late deposit, late filing of returns
attracts a per-day fee, and expense disallowance can increase your taxable
income. Timely compliance is far cheaper than regularisation.

## What we need from you

- TAN details (we can obtain TAN if you don't have one)
- Monthly payment details: nature of payment, payee PAN, amount
- Challan copies of taxes deposited
- For salary TDS: employee declarations and investment proofs
