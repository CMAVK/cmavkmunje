# Received a Tax Notice? What To Do

## First — don't panic, don't ignore

Most notices are routine (mismatch, verification, information requests) and
are resolved with a proper, timely reply. Ignoring a notice is the costliest
mistake — deadlines pass quickly and ex-parte orders or penalties can follow.

## Steps to take immediately

1. Note the **deadline** on the notice — everything is planned around it.
2. Do not reply in a hurry or admit anything without professional review.
3. Send us a copy right away — via this chat's 📎 attach button, the website
   upload page, WhatsApp or email.
4. Gather the underlying records (returns, invoices, bank statements) —
   we'll tell you exactly which ones once we read the notice.

## Common notice types we handle

- **Income tax:** 143(1) intimations and mismatches, scrutiny/assessment
  notices, reassessment, TDS demands, penalty proceedings, rectification and appeals
- **GST:** GSTR mismatch (ASMT-10), show-cause notices/demands, ITC-related
  queries, registration show-cause (REG-17), e-way bill detentions, refunds and appeals
- **MCA/ROC:** non-filing notices, strike-off notices, director KYC defaults
- **PF/ESIC:** inspections, damages and interest proceedings

## How we work on a notice

We study the notice and your records, explain your actual exposure in plain

file it and represent you before the authority where needed — one desk for
