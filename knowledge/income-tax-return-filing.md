# Income Tax Return (ITR) Filing — Documents and Process

## Who should file

Filing is compulsory when income exceeds the basic exemption limit, and in
several other cases regardless of income — e.g. foreign travel or electricity
expenses above prescribed limits, large deposits, foreign assets, or when TDS
has been deducted and a refund is due. Even when not compulsory, filing helps
with loans, visas and refunds.

## Documents we need from you — Salaried

- PAN and Aadhaar
- Form 16 from employer(s)
- Salary slips (if Form 16 is pending)
- Interest certificates / bank statements
- Investment and deduction proofs (LIC, PPF, ELSS, mediclaim, home-loan interest certificate)
- Details of any share/mutual-fund sales (capital gains statements from broker)
- AIS/26AS — we download and reconcile these ourselves

## Documents we need — Business / Profession

- Bank statements for the full year (all accounts)
- Sales and purchase details / books of account
- GST returns filed during the year (we reconcile turnover)
- Loan statements, asset purchase details
- Previous year's ITR and financial statements

## Old vs new tax regime

The right regime depends entirely on your numbers. We compute tax both ways
for every client and recommend the one that minimises tax while keeping your
investment goals in mind.

## Due dates (typical — confirm current year with us)

- Individuals and non-audit cases: 31 July
- Audit cases: 31 October
- Belated/revised returns: generally up to 31 December of the assessment year

Late filing attracts fees and interest, and certain losses cannot be carried
forward — filing on time always pays.

## Our process

1. Share documents (chat upload, client portal, WhatsApp or office visit).
2. We prepare the computation and share it for your confirmation, including
   regime comparison and refund/tax payable position.
3. Return is filed and e-verified; you receive the acknowledgment (ITR-V)
   and a copy of the computation for your records.
